# Create progression

Project Commonwealth gates its technology through Create. Power and machines advance in tiers: each tier unlocks the
next, and the cost of progress is paid in finding scarce resources and building deeper production chains,
not in waiting out timers. This page is the player's map of that climb.

## How the climb feels

The early tiers go quickly once you have the materials, and the back half is where the real investment
sits. A focused player can reach a steam-powered base in a day or two. Reaching electricity takes
considerably longer, because the chains run deeper and the inputs are spread across regions you have to
explore or trade for. Nothing blocks you with a cooldown; you are gated only by what you have found and
what you have built.

## The power ladder

Power is a progression, not a free pick. Each rung needs the one below it.

1. **Manual** — the hand crank bootstraps your first machines.
2. **Water and wind** — water wheels and windmills are your first real, continuous power. A windmill costs
   sail cloth and a windmill bearing; it is the first tier that asks for an investment.
3. **Steam** — the mid-game workhorse. A steam engine needs the full fluid and heat chain — pump, boiler,
   and a heat source you build. Blaze burners give no heat while idle in this pack: the basic burner
   provides low heat for everyday cooking and your first boilers, and blaze burners with blaze cakes
   carry the hotter recipes. Building that chain is the gate.
4. **Electricity (basic)** — alternators and motors convert your kinetic power into a form you can wire and
   move. Electricity is a *converter*, not a free source: its generators are driven by your steam-tier
   machinery, so you still had to climb the earlier rungs to reach it.
5. **Electricity (high)** — the reactor is the end-game tier. Its fuel rods are sheathed in metal won
   from an end-game boss, and its structure is built from the full industrial chain.

## Tiers at a glance

| Tier | Power | What you are building | How you reach it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Manual** | Hand crank | First machines, andesite parts | Free — starting tech |
| **Water/Wind** | Water wheel, windmill | Brass, electron tubes, a basic hot-air balloon | Sail cloth + windmill bearing |
| **Steam** | Steam engine | Precision mechanisms, steel, a propelled balloon you steer by hand | Build the fluid and heat chain, starting with a basic burner |
| **Electric (low)** | Alternator, motors | Circuits, capacitors, complex flight controls | Drive electricity with steam-tier kinetic |
| **Electric (high)** | Reactor | End-game machines, industrial vein drilling, and large automated airships | Defeat bosses for the gate materials |

## Cost and complexity

Basic fabricated parts cost roughly three times their raw materials, so the things you make constantly
have real weight. Higher-tier items do not simply cost more — they pass through *more steps*, often pulling
in components from across the pack's mods. A more complex machine is a longer chain, not a bigger pile of
the same ingredient.

Each tier also has a telltale component that its gear is built around. Steam-tier work runs on the basic
burner and steel; the electric tier's mark is the capacitor — which itself takes energised iron, real
proof of a powered base — and you will meet it in jetpacks, power gear, and the better firearms; the top
tier asks for boss metals, and its generator magnets take silver, which only the occult smelting route
produces. If a recipe asks for one of these, that is the game telling you what tier you are stepping into.

See also: [weapons-and-gear](weapons-and-gear.md) for the full equipment picture, and
[prospecting](prospecting.md) for finding ore.

## Flight

Aircraft advance alongside the power ladder, gated by how much control you can manage:

- **Water/Wind:** a basic hot-air balloon. It lifts; it does not steer.
- **Steam:** a propelled balloon driven by a heavy steam engine, with your first hands-on analog controls.
  This is where you learn how control systems work.
- **Electric (low):** complex analog control — gyroscopic bearings and control surfaces for genuinely
  maneuverable craft.
- **Electric (high):** digital control, which makes large and complex flying machines practical to fly.

## The boss gates

From the lower electric tier onward, key machines call for materials won from specific bosses, scaled to
the tier: a mid-tier trophy unlocks advanced electrics, the airship core asks for metal from a tougher
fight, and the reactor's fuel rods take metal from an end-game boss. The gates are spread across several
boss mods, so the climb as a whole never funnels through one fight — but each gate names its boss, so
expect to gear up for the hunt that matches your tier. A later update will add an optional
settlement-based route that bypasses individual boss requirements for players who prefer to build rather
than fight.

## End-game extraction

Create: Ore Excavation's drilling machine taps ore veins industrially. It is an end-game build — its
machines call for circuit boards and electric motors from the top of the industrial chain, and running it
consumes steel drill heads, heavy rotational power, and fluid. Finding veins is not gated: the sample
drill, vein finder, and vein atlas are available from the mid tiers, so you can prospect and plan long
before you can drill.
