# Weapons and Gear

Project Commonwealth's combat equipment is built, not found. Guns, jetpacks, exoskeletons, and capture
tools all come off the same Create production lines as the rest of your base, and each sits at a definite
place on the tech ladder. This page covers what exists and what it takes to build it.

## Firearms

The pack's guns are the Create: Armorer set — Create-themed pistols, shotguns, rifles, snipers, and
heavier ordnance. There is no gun bench: every weapon assembles on the **Mechanical Crafter** from
manufactured components, and the components are factory work in their own right.

- **Gun barrel** — machined from steel on the Crafter.
- **Firing mechanism** — built from a deployer, cogwheels, a rotation speed controller, and flint and
  steel.
- **Gun trigger** — brass sheets around a precision mechanism.
- **Primer** — andesite alloy filled with gunpowder fluid at a spout.

Scopes and grips are Create work too — the telephoto sniper scope carries an electron-tube rangefinder —
and the propellant line feeds the artillery as well: cannon powder charges fill from the same gunpowder
fluid, and the 40mm high-explosive shell is finished with a Create Big Cannons impact fuze.

The weapons themselves climb the tiers:

| Tier | Weapons | What they ask for |
|---|---|---|
| Early | Both pistols, the melee wrench | Components + brass and wood |
| Self-loading | SMG, both shotguns, both rifles, both snipers, the atomic melee | Components + a **recoil spring** from Create Big Cannons |
| Heavy ordnance | The machine gun, the grenade launcher, the 40mm field cannon | Components + a **steel autocannon barrel** from Create Big Cannons |

A self-loading action cycles on its recoil spring, and the autocannon barrel comes out of Create Big
Cannons' cast-and-bore steel line — the pack's firearms grow out of the artillery industry, so expect to
build a Big Cannons workshop on the way to a serious armory.

## Ammunition

Ammunition is a four-stage production line, not a craft. Brass casings are cut from sheets; each round
then takes a **primer** (deployed), a **gunpowder fill** (spout), a **lead projectile** (deployed —
nuggets for small arms, ingots for the 40mm and grenade shells), and a final **crimping press**. A
working armory needs a standing supply of brass, gunpowder, and lead — the lead veins are not optional
for a gunfighter.

## Jetpacks

There is one jetpack line: **Create Jetpack**. The base jetpack is an electric-age build — brass plates,
a chute, a copper backtank, an elytra, a shaft, a precision mechanism, and a capacitor, assembled on the
Mechanical Crafter. A fresh jetpack starts empty; fill it with compressed air like any backtank. The
**netherite jetpack** is the top-tier upgrade and asks for cursium won from Maledictus — there is no
smithing-table shortcut.

## Exoskeletons and power gear

Create Stuff 'N Additions provides the wearable power gear, and its engines are built into the heat
ladder: the **heat engine** is assembled around a basic burner, and the **steam engine** takes steel.

- **Exoskeletons** climb andesite → copper → brass. The andesite and copper models are reachable before
  electricity; the brass exoskeleton — the strength-and-haste flagship — is an electric-age build with
  capacitors in its engine row.
- The rest of the power gear is built from the machine it works like, each on the Mechanical Crafter:
  the **portable drill** carries a real drill head from Create: Ore Excavation, the **flamethrower** is
  built around a TFMG steel fluid tank, the **grappling whisk** launches from an aeronautics plunger
  launcher, and the **fan component** spins on a Crafts & Additions electric motor.

## Capture tools

Mob lassos are late-game equipment, priced for what they do:

- **Golden lasso** — the base: Create electronics (electron tubes, a precision mechanism), gold, and an
  ender pearl.
- **Aqua** and **emerald** lassos branch off the golden with prismarine-and-fish and emeralds-and-brass;
  the **contract** for villager work is sealed with a brass sheet.
- **Hostile lasso** — capturing monsters takes a monster's trophy: a monstrous horn from the Ancient
  Remnant, plus blaze rods and obsidian plates.
- **Diamond lasso** — the master tool merges a golden, an aqua, and a hostile lasso with diamonds and
  one boss trophy of your choice.

Bosses themselves cannot be lassoed, by any tier.

## Where the gates are

If a recipe asks for a **capacitor**, you need a powered base. If it asks for a **recoil spring** or an
**autocannon barrel**, you need a Big Cannons workshop. If it asks for a **boss material** — cursium,
ignitium, a monstrous horn, or a trophy of your choice — the game is sending you hunting. The gates are
the map: gear up for the fight that matches the gear you want.
